## 2026-07-14 ‚Äî Bike rig (PLAN-02 task 2) design choices
- **Choice:** (a) Gas/brake/air-control/assist are implemented as per-step angular-VELOCITY shaping (`scene.matter.setAngularVelocity` fed by pure control-law functions), not literal Matter torques ‚Äî a fixed spin-up per tick IS the torque limit, and pure functions are unit-testable in Node. (b) The head fail-sensor is an `isSensor` PART of the chassis compound body (near-zero density), not a separate constrained body ‚Äî rigid, zero dynamics impact, no extra constraint to leak. (c) `airborneRotation` resets at TAKEOFF, not landing, so PLAN-07 trick detection can read a finished flip's total any time after touchdown. (d) Brake wins over gas when both pedals are held (safer for a non-gamer mashing both). (e) The rear wheel keeps being driven while airborne (no reaction torque on the chassis; a spinning wheel bites on landing). (f) A crashed bike "goes limp" (input + assist ignored, physics keeps tumbling) rather than freezing, until the scene destroys/recreates it. (g) Added `bikeSpawnY()` + `BIKE_TUNING.spawnClearancePx` so the scene spawns with a small air gap ‚Äî guards the terrain caveat that the physics chain can sit slightly above `heightAt(x)` in troughs. (h) `'terrain'` label became the exported `TERRAIN_BODY_LABEL` constant in terrain.ts, imported by bike.ts.
- **Why:** PLAN-02's task text specifies behaviors, not mechanisms; none of this touches personal content. The velocity-shaping approach keeps every feel-critical number a single commented `BIKE_TUNING` knob for the task-6 tuning pass, and reset-at-takeoff is the only reset timing that lets a polling scene reliably observe a completed flip.
- **How chosen:** Simplest options that keep the game EASY (assist mandatory, torque-limited gas, forgiving crash model) and testable without faking physics (Rule 3, CLAUDE.md).

## 2026-07-14 ‚Äî Trick-input mechanic: mid-air pedal presses get full pitch authority; held-from-ground pedals ramp in late (PLAN-02 task 6)
- **Choice:** Airborne pitch authority is gated by input history (new pure functions in bike.ts: `nextPedalAirFresh`, `airPitchAuthority`, `isTrickAirPhase`, `nextHeldSteps`; knobs `heldPitchDelaySteps`/`heldPitchRampSteps`/`trickGroundDebounceSteps`/`trickPressBufferSteps`): a pedal press that begins mid-air (or within 0.2s before takeoff ‚Äî "press buffering") gets authority 1 instantly; a pedal held since the ground gets 0 for the first 0.7s airborne, then ramps to 1 (so NORTH_STAR ¬ß4's "gas also rotates nose-up in mid-air" stays true on genuinely long airtime). At partial/zero authority the pedal result is blended with the auto-stabilization assist, so a gas-holding non-gamer gets the full assist on every hop.
- **Why:** The acceptance criteria are jointly impossible without an input-history distinction: "gas-only over the big ramp does NOT flip and does NOT crash" and "hold gas in the air off the same ramp = backflip" are the same physical input unless the game can tell a deliberate mid-air press from a cruise-hold. Browser-measured along the way: (1) a purely ballistic gas-held hop (first attempt: zero authority = no assist either) let natural takeoff spin build to ~90¬∞ and crash ‚Äî hence the assist blend; (2) crest wheel-chatter on the coarse collision chords ate mid-air presses (freshness cleared by 1-3-step ground touches) ‚Äî hence the 10-step ground debounce; (3) requiring the press strictly after takeoff cost ~140¬∞ of rotation to reaction latency ‚Äî hence the 12-step press buffer.
- **How chosen:** Only design found that satisfies NORTH_STAR ¬ß4's control description, "a non-gamer never accidentally flips", and "flips achievable but optional" simultaneously; every piece is a commented BIKE_TUNING knob + unit-tested pure function (Rule 3, CLAUDE.md).

## 2026-07-15 ‚Äî Held touch-pedal state is cleared on scene RESUME so pausing can't leave the gas stuck on (PLAN-03 integration fix)
- **Choice:** GameScene registers a `Phaser.Scenes.Events.RESUME` handler (`onResume`, paired with an `off` in the SHUTDOWN handler so it can't stack across restarts) that clears both input touch flags and resets both pedal visuals (`gameInput.setTouchGas/Brake(false)` + new `PedalsHandle.releaseAll()`). Esc/P were also made to resume from the pause menu (handled inside PauseScene, since a paused GameScene's `update()` doesn't run).
- **Why:** Found by the final holistic review. The pause menu uses `scene.pause()`, and a PAUSED Phaser scene stops dispatching pointer events ‚Äî so a touch pedal HELD into the pause, then released while the menu is open, never receives its `pointerup`, and would resume STUCK-ON (phantom gas) until the next tap. That directly breaks the pedals' signature "genuine press/release edges, no stuck pedal" guarantee on the actual phone. Resetting on RESUME is the safe direction: a genuinely-still-held finger simply needs a re-press (the same already-accepted behavior as the fail-restart path). (The portrait-overlay auto-pause path is unaffected ‚Äî it sleeps the RAF loop with the scene still RUNNING, so DOM events queue and drain on wake.) Committed with a red‚Üígreen touch-context regression assertion in `scripts/playtest-pause.mjs` (stubbed handler: resume `gas:true`, driven 302px; fixed: `gas:false`, coast-only 85px).
- **How chosen:** Reviewer-recommended minimal fix that restores the invariant without touching the genuine-edge path during normal play (the handler fires only on scene resume) ‚Äî evidence before assertion (Rule 2/3, CLAUDE.md).
